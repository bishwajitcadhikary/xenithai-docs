# OAuth - Social Login

### Step 1: Go to OAuth Page
1. Log into your Admin Panel and navigate to the "Settings" section.
2. Click on the "OAuth" tab.
[![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/1.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/1.png)

### Step 2: Enable OAuth
1. Select the "Enable Social Login" selection box.
2. Click on the "Save" button.
<div className="bg-img">
    [![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/2.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/2.png)
</div>

### Step 3: Set OAuth Credentials
#### Facebook
1. Go to [Facebook Developers](https://developers.facebook.com/) and log in.
2. Click on the "My Apps" button and select "Add a New App".
3. Select "Website" as the platform.
4. Enter a name for your app.
5. Click on the "Create New Facebook App ID" button.
6. Select a category for your app.
7. Click on the "Create App ID" button.
8. Click on the "Skip Quick Start" button.
9. Click on the "Settings" tab.
10. Copy the "App ID" and "App Secret" into the "Client ID" and "Client Secret" fields.
11. Click on the "Save" button.
* Callback URL: `https://<your-domain>/oauth/facebook/callback`
    [![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/3.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/3.png)

#### Google
1. Go to [Google Developers Console](https://console.developers.google.com/) and log in.
2. Click on the "Create Project" button.
3. Enter a name for your project.
4. Click on the "Create" button.
5. Click on the "Enable and Manage APIs" button.
6. Click on the "Credentials" tab.
7. Click on the "Create Credentials" button and select "OAuth client ID".
8. Select "Web application" as the application type.
9. Enter a name for your OAuth client.
10. Enter the following URL into the "Authorized JavaScript origins" field: `https://<your-domain>`
11. Enter the following URL into the "Authorized redirect URIs" field: `https://<your-domain>/oauth/google/callback`
12. Click on the "Create" button.
13. Copy the "Client ID" and "Client Secret" into the "Client ID" and "Client Secret" fields.
14. Click on the "Save" button.
[![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/4.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/4.png)

#### Twitter
1. Go to [Twitter Apps](https://apps.twitter.com/) and log in.
2. Click on the "Create New App" button.
3. Enter a name for your app.
4. Enter a description for your app.
5. Enter the following URL into the "Website" field: `https://<your-domain>`
6. Enter the following URL into the "Callback URL" field: `https://<your-domain>/oauth/twitter/callback`
7. Click on the "Create your Twitter application" button.
8. Click on the "Keys and Access Tokens" tab.
9. Click on the "Create my access token" button.
10. Copy the "Consumer Key (API Key)" and "Consumer Secret (API Secret)" into the "Client ID" and "Client Secret" fields.
11. Click on the "Save" button.
[![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/5.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/5.png)

#### LinkedIn
1. Go to [LinkedIn Developers](https://www.linkedin.com/developer/apps) and log in.
2. Click on the "Create Application" button.
3. Enter a name for your app.
4. Enter a description for your app.
5. Enter the following URL into the "Website URL" field: `https://<your-domain>`
6. Enter the following URL into the "OAuth 2.0 Redirect URLs" field: `https://<your-domain>/oauth/linkedin/callback`
7. Click on the "Submit" button.
8. Copy the "Client ID" and "Client Secret" into the "Client ID" and "Client Secret" fields.
9. Click on the "Save" button.
[![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/6.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/6.png)

## Step 4: Test OAuth
1. Log out of your Admin Panel or open a new incognito window.
2. Click on the "Login" button or navigate to the "Login" page.
3. Click on the "Login with Facebook" button.
4. Click on the "Login with Google" button.
5. Click on the "Login with Twitter" button.
6. Click on the "Login with LinkedIn" button.
[![image](/images/admin/settings/oauth/7.png)](/images/admin/settings/oauth/7.png)
