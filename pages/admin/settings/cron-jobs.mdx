# Cron Jobs

Here you can manage the cron jobs that are available in your application.

### Step 1: Go to Cron Jobs Page
1. Log into your Admin Panel and navigate to the `Settings` section.
2. Click on the `Cron Jobs` tab.
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/1.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />

### Step 2: Using single Command
1. Click on the `Copy` button.
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/2.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />
2. Log into your cPanel and navigate to the `Cron Jobs` section.
3. Paste the command into the `Command` field.
4. Set the frequency of the cron job.
5. Click on the `Add New Cron Job` button.
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/3.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} /> <br/>
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/4.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />

It will look like this:
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/5.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />

### Step 3: Using multiple Commands
For example, I will show you how to set up the cron job for the `Send Subscription Expiration Notice` command.
1. Click on the `Copy` button.
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/6.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />
2. Log into your cPanel and navigate to the `Cron Jobs` section.
3. Paste the command into the `Command` field.
4. Set the frequency of the cron job.
5. Click on the `Add New Cron Job` button.
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/7.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} /> <br/>
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/8.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />

### Step 4: Repeat Step 3 for all the commands
So, you will have 5 cron jobs in total.
<Image src={'/images/admin/settings/cron-jobs/9.png'} className={'bg-img'} width={1000} height={500} alt={'Image'} />

### Step 5: Regenerate the Token
1. Click on the `Regenerate Token` button.

Note: If you regenerate the token, you will need to update the cron jobs in your cPanel.
